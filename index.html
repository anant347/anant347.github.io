Assessing flight path vulnerabilities over the himalayan terrain

A computational system was developed linking the weather simulation model, WRF, and the CFD software, Star-CCM+, to assess the affect of any weather system on an aircraft. A system demonstration was carried out using a case study focused on scenic mountain flights from Kathmandu to Mt. Everest and back. As the himalayan region experiences one of the most intense thunderstorms in the world and as an increase in global warming is predicted to increse thunderstorm activity around the world, the case study focused on the flight of the beechcraft 1900D aircraft through a Cumulonimbus cloud that formed over Kathmandu on the 10th of July.
